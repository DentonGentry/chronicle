Chapter 10: The Tangled Web We Weave

he desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

That thought kept repeating through Ferranifer's mind, even as she learned to control the awkwardness of this new halfling body.

The desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

Even as she discovered that those short, pudgy fingers she now despised were extremely dextrous, with a well-developed sense of touch approaching that of her former vampiric body, the thirst for revenge against those who had dared defy Acerak gnawed at her.

The desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

Ferranifer had even attempted to catalog the contents of the voluminous Bag of Holding, though she gave up the effort after some of the thiefly equipment it held proved dangerous, slashing her hand badly. Kahlen healed the damage with only a quizzical nod.

The desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

The bag at the base of the stairs bore the mark of the Black Academy. It also bore Drake's mark. Drake was an instructor at the Black Academy. Revealing nothing of the rage boiling within her, she carefully placed the bottle of "Corpse dust, Acerak" in the Bag of Holding, that she might restore it to its rightful place on her return to the Prime. Drakes ashes, by contrast, would be scattered to the wind.

The desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

Her first chance to exact revenge came at the tower of the emerald spiders. She still possessed the knowledge of her previous incarnation as a necromancer and vampire, including spells to manipulate and control the undead. Taking control of five spiders, she directed them during the battle in a coordinated attack against Rosvenir. Unfortunately the spiders were too weak to be of much use, and were easily defeated by the party.

The desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

However, the battle with the spiders proved valuable, for it provided the final clue to the mystery of Kurik's condition. None of the other party members seemed to have noticed it, but Ferranifer knew the signs. He had not been eating much from the party's communal cooking. Kahlen's spells of healing were becoming less effective on him. She hadn't put the clues together until the battle with the spiders.

The desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

During the battle Rosvenir activated the Wand of Days, bathing the spiders and the immobilized Kurik in the brightest sunlight. Ferranifer instinctively cringed back, fearful of the damage which her vampiric body would have sustained. Perhaps that is why she noticed it: Kurik was smoldering. The burn damage he suffered was not from the spiders. Kurik was a vampire.

The desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

Ferranifer did not know that Kurik had disappeared during the party's trek through the Great Swamp, nor of his sudden reappearance at the Black Academy. However, she did know that XXXXXXX, a vampire and an instructor at the Academy, had disappeared several days before her own defeat. She could therefore only guess that XXXXX had captured Kurik, Embraced him as a vampire, but then been destroyed by the paladin. Though it was extremely rare for an elder vampire to be defeated by a newly created vampire, it was not unknown. The elder's control of his creations is by no means absolute.

Ferranifer did not understand how Kurik still held the favor of Pandeos. He was able to Repel the wight in the library, and his spells of blessing and protection were as strong as ever. This meant he was still behaving as a Paladin, and had to be treated carefully.

The desecrators of the Tomb must pay.

For the first time since taking over this hairy body, things were looking up. The beginning of a plan began to take shape. She could gain the favor of Acerak, her deity, by destroying this group of interlopers. She would become a vampire again with Kurik's help, willing or no. Things were definately looking up.
